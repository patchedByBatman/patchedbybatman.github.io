<p>
Chandra Mouli Gunturi is a top-graduating MSc Electronics (2024) student from Queen's University Belfast, where he developed a novel quad-copter altitude control algorithm for his final project. He received a first class undergrad degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering (E.E.E) from P.V.P Siddhartha Institute of Technology in 2021.

He interned at Cummins Inc as an Electronic Systems Engineer from 2023 to 2024, where he contributed to the development of a Wireless Sensor Control Unit (W-SCU), Active Cell Balancing Circuit amongst other projects. Whilst interning at Cummins, he also worked on Electronic Parking Brake (EPB) systems and authored a Cummins Technical Report on optimal design of temperature measurement circuits. 

He is currently working at Randox Laboratories Ltd, as an Electrical and Electronics Design Engineer. Where his responsibilities include designing Electrical and Electronic systems up to various internationally recognised standards, designing analogue and digital circuits, and developing industrial automation solutions using Siemens PLCs.

His career interests span the fields of Applied Mathematics, Control Engineering, Autonomous Robotics, Swarms, Electrical, Electronics and Computer Science engineering.
